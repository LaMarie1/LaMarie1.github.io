How to Build Your Own Hive

1) Bee on the Right Side of Your Local Laws

Check any regulations or ordinances in your community concerning beekeeping. Make sure you meet your stateâ€™s regulatory requirements concerning the liscence you need to beekeep. Don't forget to get your hives approved.

2) Where to Put Your Behive

A Secluded part of a Garden If you live in a city and happen to have you're own garden, this is a perfect spot. If not, check to see if there is a community garden close by or if aneighbor is willing to let you use theirs.

Balcony or Roof

If you live in an aprtment with an unused balcony or roof, you can place your hive here

Indoors

Although this is not the most traditoinal method, it is still possible. The hives are usually attached to an wall, with come sort of small opening that allows the bees to get outside.

3) Get Supplies

These materials are necessary for builing a urban bekeeping. *Some listed below apply more to honey bees.

Hive Box

Find a box that suits your enviroment best.

Hive Stand

This is important, as raises the hive above the ground. This gives provides sufficient air flow and ventelation, preventing moisture damage to the hive.

Bottom Board

The hive sits on a bottom board, which can be either mesh ot solid. Solid boards heep the hive warm during the winter and prevents ants, a predator to bees, from entering the hive. Mesh boards prevent an accumilation of mites (wich fall off of bees when they clean themselves) on the bottom of the hive.

Deep Hive Body

This internal frame houses the bees and holds the honeycombs.

Frames

Bees use these foundations (which can be wooden or plastic) to build their honeycombs.

Shallow Chamber

This chamber collects the surplus of honey.

Inner and Outer Cover

These help with air circulation.

Feeders

Bees cannot collest enough nectar during the winter, so feeders provides them with the necessary sugar and water needed to sustain themselves.

Smoker

This is an extremely helpful yool that calms the bees down, making it easier to harvest honey.

Frame Lifter

This helps with getting the frames out of the hives.

Bee-Proof Suit

This gives you with protection when handling the bees or their habitat.

A swarm of Bees with a Queen

You obviously cannot have a beehive without bees. Make sure there is a queen so that bees can continue to be produced and you can have a flourishing and thriving hice at all times.

4) Build Your Hive

Once you have all of the supplies, you just have to put verything together. Unless you have some training in this it might be best to find a professional to install your hive.
